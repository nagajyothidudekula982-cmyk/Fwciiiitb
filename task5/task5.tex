\noindent
\textbf{Question:}

What are the minimum number of 2-to-1 multiplexers required to generate a 2-input AND gate and a 2-input Ex-OR gate?

\begin{enumerate}
\item[(A)] 1 and 2
\item[(B)] 1 and 3
\item[(C)] 1 and 1
\item[(D)] 2 and 2
\end{enumerate}

\vspace{0.5cm}

\noindent
\textbf{Solution:}

A 2-to-1 multiplexer has:

\[
Y = \bar{S}I_0 + SI_1
\]

where:

\begin{itemize}
\item $S$ = Select line
\item $I_0, I_1$ = Inputs
\item $Y$ = Output
\end{itemize}

\vspace{0.3cm}

\noindent
\textbf{1. AND Gate using one 2-to-1 MUX}

For AND gate:

\[
Y = A \cdot B
\]

Choose:

\[
S = A
\]

\[
I_0 = 0
\]

\[
I_1 = B
\]

Then:

\[
Y = \bar{A}(0) + A(B)
\]

\[
Y = AB
\]

Hence, only \textbf{1 multiplexer} is required.

\vspace{0.5cm}

\noindent
\textbf{2. EX-OR Gate using 2-to-1 MUX}

For XOR gate:

\[
Y = A \oplus B
\]

Choose:

\[
S = A
\]

\[
I_0 = B
\]

\[
I_1 = \bar{B}
\]

Then:

\[
Y = \bar{A}B + A\bar{B}
\]

\[
Y = A \oplus B
\]

To obtain $\bar{B}$, one additional 2-to-1 MUX is required.

Therefore, total multiplexers required:

\[
2
\]

\vspace{0.5cm}

\noindent
\textbf{Final Answer:}

\[
\boxed{\text{(A) 1 \text{ and } 2}}
\]
